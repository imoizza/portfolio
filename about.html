About

Engineer by training,
designer by conviction

I spent four years learning to build things, then realised the harder question was what to build. So I switched sides of the table.

I'm Moizza Azhar, a product designer currently based in Orsay, France, where I'm doing a master's in Human-Computer Interaction at Université Paris-Saclay.

My route here wasn't the usual one. I did a computer science degree at UET Lahore, which means I can read the code my designs turn into — a genuinely unfair advantage in handoff meetings, and the reason I take engineering constraints as a design input rather than an obstacle.

For two years at SmartEnds I designed 15+ digital platforms, including five client-critical IoT systems monitoring over a thousand physical devices. That work taught me the thing I now care most about: dense, technical data is a design problem, not an excuse. An engineer reading a sensor dashboard at 3am deserves the same care as anyone using a consumer app.

What I'm actually good at

Taking something complicated and structured — sensor networks, financial flows, multi-step onboarding — and finding the shape that makes it obvious. I build design systems that survive contact with real engineering teams, and I prototype early enough that the arguments happen in Figma instead of in production.

How I work

I ask a lot of questions at the start, which slows the first week down and speeds up every week after. I test with real people before I trust my own instincts. And I write things down, because a decision nobody can find is a decision that gets made twice.
